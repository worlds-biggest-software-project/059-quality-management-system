## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **ISO 9001:2015** — Core international quality management standard applicable to virtually all industries; mandates process approach, risk-based thinking, and CAPA.
- **ISO 13485:2016** — Medical device QMS standard; specifies requirements for organizations in the medical device design and manufacturing supply chain.
- **ISO 14971:2019** — Medical device risk management standard; central to risk analysis workflows in medical device QMS.
- **IATF 16949:2016** — Automotive quality management standard (replaces ISO/TS 16949); requires QMS with specific APQP, FMEA, and control plan capabilities.
- **AS9100 Rev D** — Aerospace quality management standard; extends ISO 9001 with additional requirements for aviation, space, and defense.
- **FDA 21 CFR Part 11** — US FDA regulation for electronic records and signatures in life sciences; requires validated systems with audit trails and access controls.
- **EU MDR (2017/745)** — EU Medical Device Regulation requiring robust post-market surveillance and quality system documentation for device manufacturers.
- **FDA 21 CFR Part 820 (QSR)** — Quality System Regulation for medical devices sold in the US; aligns with ISO 13485 but with US-specific requirements.
- **GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice)** — Regulatory frameworks (FDA, EMA, WHO GMP) requiring documented quality systems in pharmaceutical and food manufacturing.
- **VDA 6.3** — German automotive association process audit standard; commonly required alongside IATF 16949 in European automotive supply chains.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- **Predictive CAPA and defect prevention:** Current QMS tools are reactive — they manage CAPAs after a non-conformance occurs. AI models trained on historical production data, environmental factors, and supplier quality records can predict defect likelihood before it happens, shifting from reactive CAPA management to proactive risk elimination. This capability would reduce recall exposure significantly and is largely absent in open-source QMS tools.
- **Automated non-conformance root-cause analysis:** When a non-conformance is logged, engineers manually investigate root causes. An AI layer that automatically correlates the NCR with recent process changes, supplier lots, equipment maintenance records, and similar historical incidents — then proposes probable root causes with evidence — would compress CAPA cycle time from weeks to hours.
- **Natural language audit preparation:** Preparing for ISO or FDA audits requires collecting evidence across hundreds of document records. An AI-native QMS that can auto-generate audit packages ("produce all CAPA records closed in the last 12 months with supporting evidence for ISO 9001 Clause 10.2") would eliminate days of manual audit prep work.
- **Intelligent document control:** Change control in regulated QMS environments is painful — impacted documents must be identified, routed, and re-approved. AI that automatically identifies all documents impacted by a specification change, drafts redline amendments, and initiates the appropriate approval workflow would address a major pain point in life sciences QMS.
- **Open-source gap:** No open-source QMS combines modern UX, a proper regulatory data model (CAPA, audit, NCR, document control), and an AI layer. ProcessMaker provides workflow mechanics but no QMS structure; qmsWrapper has the QMS model but no AI and limited community. An AI-native OSS QMS with ISO 9001/13485 templates would address a significant underserved market, particularly for startups in medical devices and manufacturing that cannot afford Veeva or MasterControl.
