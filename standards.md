# Standards & API Reference

> Project: Quality Management System (QMS) · Generated: 2026-05-06

## Industry Standards & Specifications

### ISO Standards

**ISO 9001:2015 — Quality management systems: Requirements**
- Official URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/62085.html
- The foundational international quality management standard applicable to all industries. Mandates a process-based approach with Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycles, risk-based thinking, and continual improvement. Every QMS product must be capable of supporting ISO 9001 compliance evidence. A revision (ISO/FDIS 9001) is in progress with publication expected September 2026.
- Draft revision URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/88464.html

**ISO/FDIS 9001:2026 (forthcoming) — Quality management systems: Requirements**
- Official URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/88464.html
- The upcoming revision (expected Q3 2026) introduces explicit references to digitalization, AI, automation, and data analytics within QMS requirements. Organizations using AI for quality measurement, monitoring, or data analysis may need to validate those tools. This revision also addresses climate change considerations in organizational context analysis. An AI-native QMS built now is positioned ahead of this standard.

**ISO 13485:2016 — Medical devices: Quality management systems: Requirements for regulatory purposes**
- Official URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/59752.html
- The mandatory QMS standard for organizations in the medical device design and manufacturing supply chain. Incorporated by reference into the FDA's Quality Management System Regulation (QMSR, effective 2 February 2026), replacing the prior 21 CFR Part 820 Quality System Regulation. Any QMS targeting medical device companies must be designed to support ISO 13485 compliance, controlled document management, CAPA records, and internal audit evidence.

**ISO 14971:2019 — Medical devices: Application of risk management to medical devices**
- Official URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/72704.html
- Third edition; specifies requirements for the complete risk management lifecycle: risk analysis, risk evaluation, risk control, and production and post-production activities. Requires a risk management file linking hazards, risk estimates, risk mitigations, and residual risk acceptance. A QMS serving medical device manufacturers must support ISO 14971 risk file management with traceability to design outputs and verification records.

**ISO 14001:2015 — Environmental management systems: Requirements with guidance for use**
- Official URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/60857.html
- Environmental management system standard sharing Annex SL high-level structure with ISO 9001 and ISO 45001, enabling integrated management system deployments. Organizations running an integrated QMS/EMS/OHSMS expect a single QMS platform to support multi-standard compliance evidence. Corrective action and audit management modules are common across all three standards.

**ISO 45001:2018 — Occupational health and safety management systems: Requirements with guidance for use**
- Official URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/63787.html
- Occupational health and safety standard using the same Annex SL structure as ISO 9001 and ISO 14001. Hazard identification, incident management, and CAPA workflows overlap significantly with quality management processes. An integrated QMS platform that supports ISO 9001, ISO 14001, and ISO 45001 simultaneously offers significant market advantage.

**ISO 56001:2024 — Innovation management system: Requirements**
- Official URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/79278.html
- First global requirements standard for innovation management systems, published in 2024. Shares Annex SL structure with ISO 9001, enabling integration. As organizations seek to link quality and innovation governance, QMS platforms with configurable management system support will gain advantage in this emerging area.

### Regulatory Frameworks

**FDA QMSR — Quality Management System Regulation (21 CFR Part 820, effective 2 February 2026)**
- Official URL: https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/postmarket-requirements-devices/quality-management-system-regulation-qmsr
- The FDA's revised device CGMP regulation now incorporates ISO 13485:2016 by reference, replacing the prior prescriptive QSR subparts C–O. Additional FDA-specific requirements in Section 820.35 (Control of Records) and Section 820.45 (Control of Device Labeling and Packaging) supplement ISO 13485. Manufacturers must provide audit evidence of ISO 13485 compliance; FDA certificates of conformance to ISO 13485 will not be issued and will not exempt manufacturers from FDA inspections.
- eCFR URL: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-H/part-820

**FDA 21 CFR Part 11 — Electronic Records; Electronic Signatures**
- Official URL: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-11
- Governs the use of electronic records and electronic signatures in FDA-regulated industries. Requires audit trail, access controls, and validation of electronic systems used to create or modify records. Note: Part 11 electronic signature requirements do not apply directly to the QMSR (which incorporates ISO 13485, which has no signature requirements), but remain applicable for pharmaceutical and other FDA-regulated contexts. Any QMS targeting pharma must support Part 11 compliance.

**EU MDR 2017/745 — EU Medical Device Regulation**
- Official URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32017R0745
- Requires robust post-market surveillance, clinical evidence maintenance, and quality system documentation for medical device manufacturers selling in the EU. Complaint management, MDR (Medical Device Reporting) workflow, and PSUR (Periodic Safety Update Report) support are compliance requirements for EU MDR alignment. Annex IX and Annex XI specify conformity assessment procedures that rely on QMS evidence.

**GMP — Good Manufacturing Practice (FDA, EMA, WHO)**
- FDA cGMP: https://www.fda.gov/drugs/pharmaceutical-quality-resources/current-good-manufacturing-practice-cgmp-regulations
- EU GMP (EudraLex Volume 4): https://health.ec.europa.eu/medicinal-products/eudralex/eudralex-volume-4_en
- Regulatory frameworks governing pharmaceutical and food manufacturing quality systems. Electronic batch records (EBR) and production records are GMP artefacts that may be captured in or linked from a QMS. GAMP 5 (ISPE guidance) provides risk-based validation methodology for computerised systems used in GMP environments.

**IATF 16949:2016 — Quality management systems: Requirements for automotive production and relevant service parts organizations**
- Official URL: https://www.iatfglobaloversight.org/
- AIAG/IATF standard mandating automotive-specific QMS with core tools integration: APQP (Advanced Product Quality Planning), PPAP (Production Part Approval Process), FMEA, MSA (Measurement System Analysis), and SPC (Statistical Process Control). Requires customer-specific requirements (CSR) tracking per OEM. QMS platforms targeting automotive must support 18-element PPAP documentation packages and APQP phase-gate deliverables.

**AS9100 Rev D (IA9100:2026 forthcoming) — Quality management systems: Requirements for aviation, space, and defense organizations**
- Official URL: https://iaqg.org/standard/9100-qms-requirements-for-aviation-space-and-defense-organizations/
- IAQG standard extending ISO 9001 with additional aerospace requirements including first article inspection, key characteristics management, counterfeit part prevention, and configuration management. A limited revision expected in 2026, reissued as IA9100, will introduce clarifications and additional emphasis on data-driven decision making, SPC, and measurement system analysis. QMS platforms for aerospace must support IAQG OASIS database integration for certification records.

**VDA 6.3:2023 — Process Audit**
- Official URL: https://vda-qmc.de/en/aus-und-weiterbildung/vda-6-3-faq/
- German automotive association process audit standard required alongside IATF 16949 by major European OEMs (Volkswagen Group, BMW, Mercedes-Benz). Defines a turtle diagram-based process audit methodology. Software alignment with Automotive SPICE (A-SPICE) is required for embedded software development process audits.

### Data Model & API Specifications

**OpenAPI Specification 3.1.0 / 3.2.0**
- Official URL: https://spec.openapis.org/oas/v3.2.0.html
- OAI GitHub: https://github.com/OAI/OpenAPI-Specification
- The de-facto standard for describing RESTful API contracts. A QMS platform should publish its API under an OpenAPI 3.x specification to enable automated SDK generation, interactive documentation (Swagger UI), and third-party integration tooling. MasterControl, ProcessMaker, and Intelex all expose Swagger UI documentation based on OpenAPI.

**GS1 EPCIS 2.0 — Electronic Product Code Information Services**
- Official URL: https://www.gs1.org/standards/epcis
- GS1 traceability and supply chain event messaging standard. Relevant for QMS platforms serving manufacturing and pharmaceutical verticals where product genealogy, lot traceability, and chain-of-custody evidence must interoperate with supply chain systems. EPCIS 2.0 adds sensor data for cold chain and IoT process quality monitoring alongside the core "what, when, where, why, how" event model.

**HL7 FHIR R4 / R5 — Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources**
- Official URL: https://hl7.org/fhir/
- HL7 FHIR Data Exchange for Quality Measures IG: https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/davinci-deqm/
- RESTful healthcare interoperability standard. Relevant for QMS platforms serving medical device manufacturers or clinical settings where quality data must flow to or from EHR systems, registries, or FDA data submission systems. The FDA is exploring FHIR for real-world data submissions.

**ISPE GAMP 5 (second edition) — A Risk-Based Approach to GxP Compliant Computerized Systems**
- Official URL: https://ispe.org/publications/guidance-documents/gamp-records-pharmaceutical-data-integrity
- ISPE consensus guidance (not a regulation) for validating computerised systems used in GMP-regulated pharmaceutical environments. Defines software category classifications and validation documentation expectations. A QMS targeting pharma must demonstrate GAMP 5 category-appropriate validation, including installation qualification (IQ), operational qualification (OQ), and performance qualification (PQ) evidence.

### Security & Authentication Standards

**OAuth 2.0 — RFC 6749**
- Official URL: https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc6749
- Industry-standard authorization framework for secure API access delegation. All QMS APIs should use OAuth 2.0 bearer tokens for machine-to-machine integration and user-delegated access. Life sciences QMS systems must ensure token handling meets 21 CFR Part 11 audit trail requirements.

**OpenID Connect Core 1.0**
- Official URL: https://openid.net/specs/openid-connect-core-1_0.html
- Identity layer on top of OAuth 2.0, providing user authentication and SSO. Required by enterprise QMS buyers who mandate SAML 2.0 or OIDC-based SSO integration with corporate identity providers (Microsoft Entra ID, Okta, Ping Identity). Regulated environments require FIPS 140-3 compliant cryptographic implementations for OIDC tokens.

**OWASP Application Security Verification Standard (ASVS)**
- Official URL: https://owasp.org/www-project-application-security-verification-standard/
- OWASP Secure Medical Device Deployment Standard: https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/artifacts/owasp-secure-medical-devices-deployment-standard
- Provides tiered security requirements for web applications. Level 3 applies to applications handling sensitive medical data. QMS platforms serving regulated industries should target ASVS Level 2–3 compliance. The OWASP Secure Medical Device Deployment Standard (published with CSA) provides additional deployment-level guidance for clinical and regulated manufacturing contexts.

**GDPR — General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679**
- Official URL: https://gdpr-info.eu/
- Records of Processing Activities (Article 30): https://gdpr-info.eu/art-30-gdpr/
- Applies to any QMS platform handling personal data of EU residents (employee training records, audit findings, CAPA assignees, complaint reporter data). Article 30 requires a Records of Processing Activities (RoPA). QMS platforms must support data subject access requests, right to erasure (where not in conflict with regulatory record retention obligations), and lawful basis documentation. Non-compliance penalties reach €10M or 2% of global annual turnover.

## Notes

**API Maturity Gap in OSS QMS Tools:** Neither qmsWrapper nor Senaite LIMS exposes a published, versioned REST API suitable for enterprise integration. Open-source QMS tools in this space uniformly lack the API maturity of commercial counterparts. A new AI-native OSS QMS built with a first-class OpenAPI 3.x specification would fill a significant gap.

**21 CFR Part 11 and API Design:** QMS APIs in regulated life sciences environments must preserve audit trail integrity. Any write operation through the API (create/update/delete a document, close a CAPA, record a training completion) must generate the same audit trail records as equivalent UI actions. This requirement must be enforced at the service layer, not the API layer.

**ISO 9001:2026 AI Validation Requirement:** The forthcoming revision is expected to require validation of AI tools used for quality measurement, monitoring, and data analysis. An AI-native QMS must be designed from the outset with a validation framework (IQ/OQ/PQ evidence, change control over model updates) that supports ISO 9001:2026 compliance.

**Emerging: MCP Server Specifications for QMS Integration:** The Model Context Protocol (MCP) is an emerging pattern for exposing structured data to AI agents. A QMS platform exposing an MCP server interface would allow AI assistants to query CAPA records, draft audit evidence packages, and propose root cause analyses using natural language — directly addressing the AI-native opportunity identified in research.md. No existing commercial or OSS QMS exposes an MCP server interface as of May 2026.
